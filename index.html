UN2TCC

This list is meant to translate one list to the other, both politically and geographically. There are 5 different scenarios:
  1. The UN member is divided into multiple parts such as Bosnia & Herzegovina or the UK
  2. The UN member has territory in another UN member state such as Spain in Morocco. In this case it is mentioned for both UN members
  3. An un- or only partly recognized state is within the borders of a UN member, such as Kosovo within Serbia
  4. A UN member has territory that is part of its UN membership but is not connected to the majority of the territory such as Kaliningrad (RU), Hawaii (US) or the European part of Turkey
  5. Two UN members are completely surrounded by another UN member: San Marino by Italy and Lesotho by South Africa. They are mentioned in the list but marked as UN members.

References

1 The Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus is only recognized by Turkey and separated from the Republic of Cyprus by a UN Buffer Zone.
2 The State of Palestine was recognized as a non-member state on 29 November 2012 (Resolution 67/19).
3 The Sovereign Military Order of Malta, which is an entity (not a sovereign state), has observer status at the UN and maintains diplomatic relations with 107 countries. The headquarters Palazzo Malta and Villa Malta in Rome have been granted extraterritoriality by the Italian Government. It is however not part of the TCC list.
4 The Holy See holds sovereignty over the Vatican City State and maintains diplomatic relations with 180 other states. It has been an observer state since 6 April 1964 and gained all the rights of full membership except voting on 1st July 2004.
5 The Cook Islands and Niue, both associated states of New Zealand, are not members of the UN, but are members of spezialized UN agencies.
6 The Republic of Kosovo is recognized by 112 UN member states including three UN Security Council Members: France, UK and USA.
